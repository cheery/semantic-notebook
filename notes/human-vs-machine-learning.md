# Human vs Machine: Where to Direct Attention

What programmers should leave to LLMs and what to keep developing themselves.

## Leave to machines

- **Routine code production** — translating clear ideas into working code, CRUD, boilerplate, migrations, test scaffolding, most debugging
- **Information retrieval and synthesis** — searching docs, summarizing papers, explaining APIs
- **Format juggling** — converting between formats, rewriting prose, translating between languages, refactoring for style
- **Pattern-based reasoning** — applying known solutions to problems that look like known problems, textbook problems, standard architectures

## The human edge

- **Deciding what matters** — choosing what problem to solve, who to solve it for, whether it's worth solving at all
- **Taste and judgment** — which abstraction is right, when a system feels right versus merely works, knowing which 10-line solution beats the 100-line one
- **Relationships and context** — understanding what a team actually needs versus what they say they need, navigating organizational dynamics, knowing which technical debt matters
- **Creative leaps** — connecting ideas from distant domains, noticing analogies nobody else saw, the insight that comes from taking a walk
- **Responsibility** — machines can't bear responsibility; ownership and accountability are human constructs
- **Learning to learn** — the meta-skill of rapidly getting productive in a new domain; machines are fixed at deployment, humans keep upgrading

## Practical pattern

Build tools that handle mechanical parts (embedding, indexing, searching, linking). Direct attention toward deciding what to learn, what to connect, and what it means. Tools extend memory; humans direct attention.
